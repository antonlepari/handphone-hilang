# Emergency Checklist — Device Lost or Stolen

> Last Updated: 2026-06-01

> **⚠️ WARNING:** Time is critical. Every minute of delay increases the chance of unauthorized access. Follow this checklist in order.

---

## FIRST 5 MINUTES — Do These NOW

- [ ] **1. Check if the device is nearby first**
  - Call your own number from another phone — if you hear it ring, it may just be misplaced
  - Ask people nearby if they have seen it

- [ ] **2. Open Find My Device / Find My**
  - Android: Go to **https://android.com/find** or use Find Hub app on another Android device
  - iOS: Go to **https://icloud.com/find** or open Find My app on another Apple device
  - Sign in with your Google Account / Apple ID
  - Check the last known location

- [ ] **3. Enable Lost Mode / Remote Lock**
  - Android: Find Hub > Select device > **Secure Device** > add contact message and phone number
  - iOS: Find My > Select device > **Mark as Lost** > enter phone number and message

- [ ] **4. Log out of active sessions remotely**
  - Google: Go to **https://myaccount.google.com/device-activity** > sign out the missing device
  - Apple: Go to **https://appleid.apple.com** > Devices > select device > Remove from Account (note: this removes Find My access; do this AFTER securing via Find My)

- [ ] **5. Alert a trusted person**
  - Let a family member or colleague know your device is missing
  - They can assist with monitoring Find My and contacting you if the device is found

---

## FIRST 30 MINUTES — Secure Accounts

- [ ] **Change your Google Account or Apple ID password**
  - Google: https://myaccount.google.com/security > Password
  - Apple: https://appleid.apple.com > Sign-In & Security > Password

- [ ] **Change your primary email password**
  - Gmail / Outlook / other — whoever provides your main email

- [ ] **Change passwords for financial accounts**
  - Banking apps, PayPal, Venmo, Cash App, cryptocurrency wallets

- [ ] **Contact your mobile carrier to suspend your SIM**
  - This stops calls, texts, and data from working on the stolen device
  - It also stops SMS-based 2FA codes from reaching the device

| Carrier | Phone (US) |
|---|---|
| AT&T | 1-800-331-0500 |
| T-Mobile | 1-800-937-8997 |
| Verizon | 1-800-922-0204 |
| US Cellular | 1-888-944-9400 |
| For other carriers | Check carrier website or contract paperwork |

- [ ] **Enable 2FA on email and banking accounts if not already active**
  - Use authenticator app, not SMS (since your SIM may be compromised)

---

## FIRST HOUR — Formal Reporting

- [ ] **File a police report**
  - Bring: Device make/model, IMEI, serial number, date/time/location of loss, description
  - Request a report number for insurance claims
  - See: [Police Report Template](../templates/police-report-template.md)

- [ ] **Report stolen device IMEI to your carrier**
  - Carrier can blocklist the IMEI so the device cannot register on any network
  - Call carrier number above and request IMEI blacklisting

- [ ] **Contact device insurance provider** (if applicable)
  - AppleCare+ (Apple): 1-800-APL-CARE (1-800-275-2273) or https://support.apple.com
  - Carrier insurance: Check your carrier plan

- [ ] **If this is a work device: notify IT/Security team immediately**
  - See: [Corporate Device Response](../incident-response/corporate-device-response.md)

- [ ] **Decide: Remote Wipe?**
  - If recovery is NOT possible and device contains sensitive data: erase remotely
  - Android: Find Hub > Erase Device
  - iOS: Find My > Erase This Device
  - **Warning: Remote wipe is irreversible. You cannot track the device after erasing.**

---

## WITHIN 24 HOURS — Full Account Audit

- [ ] **Review Google / Apple account activity for unauthorized access**
  - Google: https://myaccount.google.com/notifications
  - Apple: https://appleid.apple.com > Sign-In & Security > Recent Activity

- [ ] **Change passwords for all accounts saved in device browser or password manager**
  - Start with: Email, banking, work accounts, social media
  - Prioritize accounts linked to saved payment methods

- [ ] **Review social media accounts for unauthorized posts or messages**

- [ ] **Check for unrecognized sign-in activity on social media accounts**

- [ ] **Enable credit monitoring** if financial data was on the device
  - Free options: Experian, Equifax, TransUnion all offer free credit monitoring
  - Consider a credit freeze if you believe identity fraud risk is high

- [ ] **Notify bank and credit card issuers** about the loss
  - Ask them to monitor for suspicious transactions
  - Consider requesting new card numbers for cards stored in digital wallets
